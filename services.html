Frequently Asked Questions

- How often should I inspect and maintain my roof?

The general rule of thumb is to inspect and maintain your roof at least once a year. Depending on the type of roof you have, its age, and where you live, you may want to check more frequently. In South Florida's climate, it's important to check for any damage after a storm or heavy winds, and any time you notice changes such as cracked or missing shingles.

- What are the benefits of having a new roof installed?

A newly installed roof is more energy efficient, helping reduce heating and cooling costs. It increases curb appeal and property value, is less prone to damage from weather and pests, and provides stable protection for your family and possessions.

- What type of material is best for my roof?

For commercial properties, metal is often the ideal choice — durable, weather-resistant, lightweight, and long-lasting. For residential properties, options range from asphalt shingles to metal and tile depending on budget and aesthetics. Our team will assess your property and recommend the best solution.

- How long will the average job take to complete?

A commercial roofing job can take anywhere from several days to several weeks depending on the complexity, size, and type of roof, as well as weather conditions. Our project management team communicates timeline updates throughout so you're never left guessing.
