Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I inspect my roof?

The general rule is at least once a year, and after any major storm or high-wind event. In South Florida's climate, twice-yearly inspections are recommended — once before hurricane season and once after. If you notice missing shingles, interior staining, or energy bill increases, don't wait.

What are the benefits of a new roof?

A new roof improves energy efficiency, curb appeal, and property value. It also reduces risk of water damage and interior damage, and a properly installed new roof comes with manufacturer warranties that protect your investment for decades.

What roofing material is best for my building?

For commercial properties, metal roofing is often the best choice — durable, low-maintenance, and wind-resistant. For residential, options range from asphalt shingles to metal to flat systems depending on your home's design and budget. Our team will assess your property and recommend the optimal solution.

How long will a commercial roofing project take?

Most commercial jobs range from a few days to several weeks depending on roof size, complexity, material availability, and weather. Our project management team works to minimize business interruption and communicates timeline updates throughout the project.

Do you work with insurance companies?

Yes — we are an insurance carrier preferred service partner. Our team works directly with adjusters and risk management to facilitate clear communication and smooth claims processes, so you're not left managing that alone.

Do you offer financing?

Yes, we offer flexible financing options. We understand roofing is a significant investment and we want to make it as accessible as possible. Contact us to discuss financing solutions that fit your budget.
