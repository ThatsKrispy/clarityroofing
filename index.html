Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I inspect and maintain my roof?

The general rule of thumb is to inspect and maintain your roof at least once a year. Depending on the type of roof you have, its age, and where you live, you may want to check for damage more frequently. In South Florida's climate, inspections before and after hurricane season are recommended. Additionally, anytime you notice changes in the condition of your roofing material, such as cracked or missing shingles, it's best to get it inspected and repaired quickly.

What are the benefits of having a new roof installed?

Having a new roof installed can provide several benefits. A newly installed roof is more energy efficient, helping to reduce heating and cooling costs. It also increases the curb appeal of your home, meaning it can help increase its value if you're looking to sell. Furthermore, a new roof is less prone to damage from weather conditions or other elements such as tree limbs and pests. Having a new roof will also help to ensure the safety of your family and possessions as it provides stable protection from outside sources.

What type of material is best for my roof?

When it comes to the best material for a commercial business roof, metal is often the ideal choice. Metal roofs are durable and can withstand all types of weather conditions, making them an excellent long-term investment. They provide superior protection from heat and fire hazards, as well as stronger resistance to winds and storms. For residential properties, options range from asphalt shingles to metal roofing depending on your budget and aesthetic preferences. Our team will assess your property and recommend the best solution.

How long will the average job take to complete?

The average commercial roofing job can take anywhere from several days to several weeks depending on the complexity of the project. Factors such as size and type of roof, as well as weather conditions can impact the length of time it takes to complete the job. Generally speaking, a flat roof replacement or large-scale repair will take longer than a small repair. Our project management team communicates timeline updates throughout so you're never left guessing.
